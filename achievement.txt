TITLE: Sahyadri Classic 2025 - Dominating Performance with 4 Podium Finishes
DESCRIPTION: Chiplun Cycling Club showcased exceptional strength at the 7th Sahyadri Classic on December 6, 2025, with 16 participants competing across multiple categories. The club secured 4 podium finishes including Atharva Bhosale (3rd in Open Category), and a complete sweep in Women's category with Dr. Manisha Waghmare (3rd), Jyoti Paranjape (4th), and Rama Karmarkar (5th). Three warriors - Prashant Dabholkar, Ankush Jangam, and Advait Kelkar - completed the brutal 4-ghat challenge (175km, 3700m+ elevation) on their first attempt.

TITLE: Kashmir to Kanyakumari (K2K) - Historic 4000+ km Journey
DESCRIPTION: Raghav Kharche and Ahamed Sheikh represented Chiplun Cycling Club in the prestigious Kashmir to Kanyakumari cycling expedition organized by Fit India Movement for Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's 150th birth anniversary. Selected among only 150 cyclists nationwide, they completed the grueling 4000+ km journey with 20,000m elevation gain from November 1-16, 2025. Cycling 250-280 km daily through extreme weather and challenging terrain, they brought national recognition to Chiplun Cycling Club.

TITLE: Team Shakti - Women's Triumph at Ironman Goa
DESCRIPTION: Three inspiring women over 45 years old from Chiplun Cycling Club formed "Team Shakti" and conquered Ironman Goa on November 9, 2025, just one month after completing Kolhapur Lohpurush. Nisha Ambekar (swimmer), Dr. Manisha Waghmare (cyclist), and Dr. Ashwini Ganpatye (runner) completed the relay despite challenges including a cycling accident mid-race. Their message: age doesn't matter, willpower does - every woman has an "Iron Woman" within her waiting to be awakened.

TITLE: Prashant Dabholkar Conquers Ironman Dream at Kolhapur
DESCRIPTION: After a year-long wait due to injury, Prashant Dabholkar fulfilled his dream by completing the Kolhapur Ironman on October 5, 2025, in an impressive 7 hours 46 minutes. He conquered the grueling triathlon consisting of 1.9km swimming (1:27), 90km cycling (3:30), and 21km running (2:46), completing it well within the 10-hour cutoff. His dedication included 6 hours of daily practice, with special focus on swimming - his weak point - with guidance from experienced triathletes, proving that persistence and focused training can turn dreams into reality.

TITLE: Manoj Bhatwadekar Achieves 5th Consecutive Super Randonneur Title
DESCRIPTION: Manoj Bhatwadekar, Vice President of Chiplun Cycling Club and founder of Sahyadri Randonneur, achieved his 5th consecutive Super Randonneur (SR) title in 2025. What makes his achievement extraordinary is that he started cycling at age 50, and at 55, continues to inspire countless cyclists with his guidance on BRM rides. His dedication includes completing two SR titles in the same year (2023-24), proving that age is just a number when passion drives you.
